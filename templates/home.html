Research

Self-interacting dark matter (SIDM) was originally proposed with the purpose to solve small scale issues, which trouble the current prevailing lambda cold dark matter (ΛCDM) paradigm a lot. It departs from the original CDM model mainly in the inner region of galaxies due to the self-interactions between dark matter particles with a light mediator. Because of this, my studies of macroscopic astrophysical systems like spiral and elliptical galaxies, may offer key information of microscopic dark matter particles. Once the self-interactions are confirmed, it would rule out many popular dark matter candidates like axions or neutralinos.

Since the Earth is following the rotation of Milky Way stellar disk and moving rapidly through the dark halo—a sea of dark matter particles, it is in principle possible to find signals from the "weak" interactions between dark matter particles and nuclei or electrons through mediators. If the mediator is much light than the dark matter particle, the event spectra is peaked towards low recoil energies different from WIMPs case. If the self-interaction is strong enough, dark matter bound states are probably to form in high energy colliders. This again offers new directions to seach. I am exploring the possibility of resolving these features in current and future experiments.

Besides what mentioned above, I am also interested in introducing new technology like machine learning to my research, due to the data informed feature for building and testing theories.
